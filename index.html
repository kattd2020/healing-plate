High-Calorie Vanilla Milkshake

Nausea Mouth Sores No Appetite Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Chemo-friendly: no raw eggs, easy on the stomach, and gentle enough for mouth sores. Served cold to help soothe nausea.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 2 scoops vanilla ice cream
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp powdered whole milk
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp pure vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients to a blender.
  2. Blend on medium for 30 seconds until smooth and creamy.
  3. Pour into a chilled glass.
  4. Sip slowly through a straw if mouth sores are present.
~620 calories 14g protein 38g fat 55g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Stir in 1 tbsp unsalted butter after blending — adds ~100 cal and richness
  • Swap milk for an extra 2 tbsp heavy cream — adds ~100 cal
  • Blend in 2 tbsp peanut or almond butter — adds ~190 cal and 7g protein
  • Use sweetened condensed milk instead of regular milk — boosts calories and sweetness
  • Add a scoop of vanilla protein powder — adds ~110 cal and 20g protein

Banana Ginger Smoothie

Nausea No Appetite Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Ginger is one of the most well-studied natural remedies for chemo-related nausea. Banana adds gentle sweetness and is easy on a sensitive stomach.

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe banana (frozen is ideal)
  • ½ cup vanilla yogurt
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • ¼ tsp ground ginger (or ½ tsp fresh, grated)
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract
  • 4–5 ice cubes

Instructions

  1. Add all ingredients to a blender.
  2. Blend on high for 45 seconds until smooth.
  3. Taste and add more honey if needed.
  4. Pour into a chilled glass and sip slowly.
~320 calories 8g protein 5g fat 63g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Add 2 tbsp peanut or almond butter — adds ~190 cal and 7g protein
  • Swap milk for heavy cream — adds ~100 cal
  • Blend in 2 tbsp powdered whole milk — adds ~80 cal and extra protein
  • Add 1 tbsp coconut oil — adds ~120 cal of healthy fat

No Appetite Liquid

Packs over 700 calories into one glass — ideal when you can only manage a few sips. The peanut butter adds protein and healthy fat without a strong flavor.

Ingredients

Mango Peach Creamsicle Smoothie

Nausea Mouth Sores Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Cold, smooth, and naturally sweet — the frozen fruit soothes mouth sores and the mild flavor is easy on a nauseous stomach. No citrus, no seeds.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup frozen mango chunks
  • ½ cup frozen peach slices
  • ½ cup vanilla yogurt
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 1 tbsp honey

Strawberry Vanilla Cream Smoothie

Nausea Mouth Sores No Appetite Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Familiar and comforting, this tastes like a strawberry milkshake. Use frozen strawberries for a cold, soothing drink safe for mouth sores.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup frozen strawberries
  • 1 scoop vanilla ice cream
  • ½ cup whole milk
  • 2 tbsp heavy cream
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Blend all ingredients on high until completely smooth.
  2. If seeds bother your mouth, pour through a fine mesh strainer.

  4. Sip slowly through a wide straw.

  • Blend in 2 tbsp cream cheese — adds ~100 cal and a cheesecake flavor
  • Add 2 tbsp powdered whole milk — adds ~80 cal and extra protein
  • Drizzle in 1 tbsp melted butter after blending — adds ~100 cal

Avocado Honey Smoothie

No Appetite Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Avocado is one of the most calorie-dense whole foods available. Mild and creamy, this smoothie delivers nearly 600 calories with a gentle, buttery flavor that won't overwhelm a sensitive palate.

Ingredients

  • 1 ripe avocado
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup vanilla yogurt
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 4–5 ice cubes
  • Pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Halve the avocado and scoop flesh into the blender.
  2. Add remaining ingredients.
  3. Blend on high for 45 seconds until silky smooth.
  4. Serve immediately — avocado browns quickly.
~580 calories 10g protein 36g fat 58g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Add 2 tbsp heavy cream — adds ~100 cal
  • Blend in 1 tbsp almond or peanut butter — adds ~95 cal and protein
  • Add 2 tbsp powdered whole milk — adds ~80 cal
  • Use coconut milk instead of whole milk for richer flavor and more fat

Blueberry Oat Smoothie

Nausea No Appetite Liquid

Serves 1  |  Ready in 5 minutes  |  Blender

Oats blend into a thick, filling base that helps settle an uneasy stomach. Blueberries are mild in flavor and gentle — no citrus, no strong acids.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup rolled oats
  • ½ cup frozen blueberries
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • ½ cup vanilla yogurt
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 1 tbsp peanut butter
  • ½ tsp vanilla extract

Instructions

  1. Soak oats in the milk for 5 minutes to soften.

  3. Blend on high for 60 seconds until smooth.
  4. Add more milk if too thick to sip comfortably.
~450 calories 14g protein 12g fat 70g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Add 2 tbsp heavy cream — adds ~100 cal
  • Increase peanut butter to 2 tbsp — adds ~95 cal and 4g protein
  • Blend in 2 tbsp powdered whole milk — adds ~80 cal
  • Add a scoop of vanilla protein powder — adds ~110 cal and 20g protein

Creamy Potato Leek Soup

Nausea Mouth Sores No Appetite Soft

Serves 4  |  Ready in 35 minutes  |  Stovetop + Blender

Bland and buttery — one of the safest soups for all chemo symptoms. Blended smooth for mouth sores, served warm (not hot) to avoid irritation.

Ingredients

  • 3 medium Yukon Gold potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 2 leeks, white and light green parts, sliced
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ½ tsp salt
  • White pepper to taste (optional)

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in a pot over medium-low heat. Add leeks and cook 8 minutes until very soft.
  2. Add potatoes and broth. Bring to a gentle simmer.
  3. Cook 20 minutes until potatoes are completely tender.
  4. Blend until silky smooth. Stir in cream.
  5. Season gently and serve warm, not hot.
~380 calories 8g protein 22g fat 38g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Stir in an extra tbsp of butter per bowl — adds ~100 cal
  • Replace some broth with heavy cream — significantly boosts fat and calories
  • Top with a swirl of sour cream — adds ~60 cal and richness
  • Blend in 2 tbsp cream cheese for a velvety texture and extra calories

Pureed Butternut Squash Soup

Nausea No Appetite Liquid

Serves 4  |  Ready in 40 minutes  |  Stovetop + Blender

Naturally sweet and very mild — easy on a nauseous stomach. The silky texture requires no chewing and goes down easily even when appetite is very low.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium butternut squash, peeled and cubed
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • ¼ tsp nutmeg
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in a pot over medium heat. Add squash and cook 5 minutes.
  2. Add broth and simmer 25 minutes until squash is very tender.
  3. Blend completely smooth.
  4. Stir in cream and nutmeg. Warm gently — do not boil.
  5. Serve at a comfortable warm temperature.
~300 calories 5g protein 18g fat 30g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Blend in 2 tbsp peanut butter — adds ~190 cal (pairs surprisingly well)
  • Stir in extra butter per serving — adds ~100 cal
  • Use full-fat coconut milk instead of cream for a different flavor and similar calories
  • Swirl in 2 tbsp sour cream per bowl — adds ~60 cal

Creamy Chicken Noodle Soup

Nausea No Appetite Soft

Serves 4  |  Ready in 35 minutes  |  Stovetop

A nourishing classic made richer with cream. The noodles are cooked extra soft and chicken is shredded fine — no tough pieces to chew. High protein to support healing.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cooked chicken, finely shredded
  • 2 cups egg noodles, cooked very soft
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 2 medium carrots, cooked very tender and diced small
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Cook noodles and carrots until very soft — a few minutes past package directions.
  2. Warm broth and butter in a pot over medium heat.
  3. Add chicken, noodles, and carrots. Simmer 5 minutes.
  4. Stir in heavy cream. Heat gently — do not boil.
  5. Serve warm. Cool slightly before eating if mouth is sensitive.
~420 calories 28g protein 18g fat 36g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Stir in 1 tbsp butter per bowl — adds ~100 cal
  • Add extra heavy cream to the pot — boosts fat significantly
  • Mix in 2 tbsp cream cheese while hot — melts in for creaminess and calories
  • Add more noodles for extra carbs and calories

Sweet Corn Chowder

Mouth Sores No Appetite Soft

Serves 4  |  Ready in 35 minutes  |  Stovetop

Naturally sweet, not acidic, and smooth enough to eat even with mouth sores. The corn is blended into a creamy base so there are no hard or sharp pieces.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups frozen sweet corn
  • 2 medium potatoes, peeled and diced small
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 3 tbsp unsalted butter
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in a pot. Add corn and cook 3 minutes.
  2. Add potatoes and broth. Simmer 20 minutes until potatoes are very soft.
  3. Blend half the soup smooth, then stir back into the pot for a creamy texture.
  4. Stir in cream and warm gently.
  5. Cool to lukewarm before serving if mouth sores are present.
~380 calories 9g protein 22g fat 40g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Add an extra tbsp butter per bowl — adds ~100 cal
  • Stir in ¼ cup shredded mild cheddar — melts in and adds ~110 cal
  • Blend in 2 tbsp cream cheese — adds ~100 cal and extra creaminess
  • Top with a spoonful of sour cream — adds ~60 cal

Broccoli Cheddar Soup

No Appetite Soft

Serves 4  |  Ready in 30 minutes  |  Stovetop

Rich, cheesy, and satisfying — great for days when you need something comforting. Blend completely smooth to make it safe if chewing is difficult. Use mild cheddar to keep the flavor gentle.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups broccoli florets, cooked very soft
  • 1 cup mild cheddar cheese, shredded
  • 3 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 2 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • ½ tsp salt

Instructions

  1. Melt butter in a pot. Whisk in flour and cook 1 minute.
  2. Add broth gradually, whisking to prevent lumps.
  3. Add cooked broccoli and simmer 10 minutes.
  4. Blend until completely smooth.
  5. Stir in cream and cheese over low heat until melted. Do not boil.
~440 calories 16g protein 30g fat 24g carbs

Make It Higher Calorie

  • Add extra shredded cheese — melts in seamlessly for more calories and protein
  • Stir in 1 tbsp butter per bowl — adds ~100 cal
  • Blend in 2 tbsp cream cheese — adds ~100 cal and extra creaminess
  • Use half-and-half instead of broth for a richer base
